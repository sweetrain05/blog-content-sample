The Impact of Images on E-commerce Sales

Images play a pivotal role in e-commerce. According to research, 93% of consumers consider visual appearance to be the key deciding factor in a purchasing decision. High-quality visuals not only attract potential buyers but also significantly enhance conversion rates. However, creating these stunning product images can be a challenge. From coordinating photoshoots to hiring models, the process can be overwhelming for many businesses. For example, a jewelry designer on Etsy may need to organize a photoshoot for every new collection, involving substantial costs and time. AI technology can simplify this process, providing high-quality images without the logistical headaches.

Key Factors of a Great E-commerce Image

A good image is one that clearly showcases the product, aligns with the brand’s image, and meets platform-specific requirements.

1. Clearly Displays the Product

High-quality images should highlight the key features of the product. For instance, a pair of earrings should be shown from multiple angles, displaying details like the clasp, material, and design intricacies. Additionally, showing how jewelry looks when worn helps customers gauge the size and fit.

2. Consistently Reflects Brand Image

Your images should reflect your brand’s aesthetic. If your brand is known for handmade, artisanal products, the images should convey a warm, authentic feel. Consistency in style across all images helps in building a recognizable brand identity.

3. Meets Specific E-commerce Platform Requirements

Different platforms have varying requirements for product images. For example, Etsy recommends using natural light and clean backgrounds, Shopify encourages high-resolution images, and Amazon requires a pure white background. Ensuring your images comply with these guidelines can improve your product’s visibility and attractiveness on these platforms.

How to Create Sales-Boosting Images

1. Model Images

To create appealing model images, it’s essential to use models that represent your target audience. For example, if you are selling clothing on Shopify, it’s beneficial to show how the product looks on different body shapes. If your target audience includes seniors, using senior models can make your products more relatable.

2. Product Images

Keep the background simple to ensure the focus remains on the product. For instance, a simple white background can make a colorful scarf stand out more prominently. If the product’s size is important, demonstrate this visually—show a bag being worn, for example. This provides a clear perspective on the product’s actual size, helping customers make informed decisions.
